# About

Hi. I'm Jeremy, and I've spent about 30 years elbow-deep in IT and programming — long enough to have strong feelings about computers and zero shame about it. Hardware, software, systems, networks, the weird glue holding it all together: I love all of it. Honestly, good code reads like poetry to me, and I will not be apologizing for that sentence.

I'm a bit of a curious George. Always have been. If something in tech makes me go "wait, how does *that* work," there goes my weekend. This blog is where I share what I figure out — homelab projects, gear and tech I'm nerding out over, process write-ups, and the occasional rabbit hole I climbed into for no good reason other than I wanted to know.

For years I did all my learning the lurker way: reading other people's articles, following smarter folks, quietly soaking it up and never giving anything back. Now I'm older, a little wiser, and a dad, and somewhere in there it clicked that it's kind of my turn. Time to share what I've built and what 30 years taught me — and selfishly, to keep learning a ton while I do it, because the moment you explain something out loud you realize how much you didn't actually understand.

It's mostly for the IT crowd: sysadmins, network and security folks, fellow tech nerds, and DIY hobbyists who like getting their hands dirty. If that's you, welcome. Grab a coffee and stick around.

(I also take photos. That's a whole other thing living at jeremychampionphoto.com, so don't expect any of that here.)
